Care Instructions

Everything you need to grow beautiful, gourmet mushrooms from your log - no experience required.

How to Care for Your Grow Log

Your grow log arrives fully colonised and ready to fruit. Just give it the right conditions and nature does the rest. Here are the four things your log needs.

Watering

Mist your log 2-3 times daily with a spray bottle, or soak it in cold water for 6-8 hours once a week. The bark should feel damp to the touch but never waterlogged. Tap water is fine - just let it sit for an hour first if your area has heavily chlorinated water.

Light

Place your log in indirect light - a kitchen worktop away from windows, a shaded shelf, or a spot that gets ambient daylight. Avoid direct sunlight, which dries the log out quickly and can harm developing pins. Mushrooms do not need strong light to grow.

Temperature

Most of our logs fruit best between 12-20 degrees Celsius. Normal room temperature works well. Avoid placing your log near radiators, ovens, or draughty windows. A slight drop in temperature at night can actually help trigger fruiting.

Humidity

Mushrooms love humidity. If your home is dry, try placing the log on a tray of damp pebbles or loosely tent it with a clear plastic bag (with holes for airflow). Kitchens and bathrooms tend to have naturally higher humidity and make great spots.

Harvesting Your Mushrooms

Once you see pins (small bumps) forming on the log, mushrooms can reach harvestable size in just 5-10 days. Here is how to harvest them properly.

  1. Watch for the right moment. Harvest when the cap edges are still slightly curled under. Once caps flatten out fully, the mushrooms are past their peak flavour.
  2. Twist and pull gently. Grip the mushroom at the base of the stem and twist gently until it detaches from the log. Do not cut with a knife - this can leave behind a stump that may rot.
  3. Harvest the whole cluster. If mushrooms are growing in a cluster, harvest them all at once rather than picking individual ones. This gives the log a clean surface for the next flush.
  4. Rest and repeat. After harvesting, let the log rest for 2-3 weeks. Continue misting lightly during this period. Most logs produce 3-5 flushes over their lifetime, with each flush appearing a few weeks apart.
  5. Store your harvest. Fresh mushrooms are best eaten within a few days. Store them in a paper bag in the fridge - never in a sealed plastic bag, as they need to breathe.
